Causes of Flooding

Rain and snowmelt: When rain or snowmelt accumulates faster than the soil can absorb it or rivers can carry it away, flooding can occur. Ice jams: Melting snow and warm rain can break the ice that forms on rivers and streams, causing ice jams that can lead to flash flooding. Storm surges: Storm surges associated with tropical cyclones can cause coastal flooding. Tsunamis: Tsunamis can cause coastal flooding. Urbanization: Rapid urban development can reduce the amount of natural land that can absorb rainfall, increasing the risk of flooding. Water control structure failure: Failure of a water control structure, such as a levee or dam, can lead to flooding. Greenhouse gases: The increase in greenhouse gases due to industrial impacts, pollution, and burning fossil fuels is a major source of man-made flooding. Heavy sediment load: When rivers carry a heavy sediment load from catchments, it can lead to flooding, erosion, and drainage congestion
